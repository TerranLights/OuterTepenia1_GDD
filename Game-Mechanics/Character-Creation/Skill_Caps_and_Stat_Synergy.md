### Skill Maximums and MACHINE Stat Synergy

#### Design Philosophy
One of the core goals of the character system in *Inner Tepenia* is to make stat investment feel meaningful and to avoid the common RPG problem where a character with a heavily dumped stat can still become world-class in skills that should logically require that stat.

In *Fallout: New Vegas*, for example, it is possible to reach maximum (100) in Science and Medicine even with 1 Intelligence. This creates a strange situation where a character who is, by all mechanical definitions, ~~retarded~~ extremely unintelligent can still hack ultra-secure terminals and perform advanced medical procedures at the highest level.

*Inner Tepenia* addresses this by tying skill potential directly to MACHINE stats.

#### Core Mechanic: Skill Maximums

Every skill has a **base maximum of 30**.

Each point invested in a relevant MACHINE stat above 1 increases the maximum value of all associated skills by **+10**.

**Formula:**
> **Skill Maximum = 30 + ((Stat Value - 1) × 10)**

**Examples:**

| MACHINE Stat Value | Skill Maximum (Associated Skills) | Notes |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------|-------|
| 1                  | 30                                | Bare minimum capability |
| 3                  | 50                                | Solid amateur level |
| 5                  | 70                                | Competent professional |
| 7                  | 90                                | Expert level |
| 8+                 | 100+                              | True mastery possible |

#### Reaching True Mastery (100+)
To be able to reach a base skill level of **100**, the associated MACHINE stat must be **8 or higher**.

This creates a clear and intentional design choice:
- Characters with low-to-mid stats in a relevant area can become very competent (70–90), but will hit a hard ceiling.
- Only characters who heavily invest in the relevant MACHINE stat (8+) can reach the absolute highest levels of skill mastery.

#### Temporary & Situational Bonuses
While the **base maximum** is capped by stats, the game supports various temporary or situational bonuses that can push a character above their normal cap for a limited time. Examples include:

- Skill books / data shards / pre-war technical manuals
- Temporary drug/chemical effects
- Specialized equipment or implants
- Environmental conditions or support from allies

These bonuses allow creative or high-risk playstyles even for characters who haven’t maxed the relevant stat, but they do not raise the character’s permanent base maximum.

#### Example: Calculation (C) and Technical Skills
**Calculation (C)** is the primary MACHINE stat associated with technical, scientific, and intellectual skills (Science, Medicine, Hacking, Engineering, etc.).

- A character with **5 Calculation** can reach a maximum of **70** in Science and Medicine.
- A character with **8 Calculation** can reach **100** in those skills.
- A character with **1 Calculation** is limited to a maximum of **30** in technical skills, making them fundamentally incapable of advanced technical work without heavy external assistance.

#### Benefits of This System
- **Meaningful stat investment**: Dumping a stat has real, noticeable consequences.
- **Build identity**: Characters feel distinct based on their stat distribution.
- **Roleplaying support**: It becomes much harder to play a “genius hacker doctor” while also having 1–2 in Calculation.
- **Balanced progression**: High skill levels require deliberate investment rather than being available to every character.
- **Speedrunning & optimization**: Players who want to rush high-skill checks must invest points accordingly at character creation.

#### Implementation Notes
- Skill checks and crafting/repair requirements should take both the **current skill value** *and* the **stat-capped maximum** into account where appropriate.
- Some high-level content (advanced terminals, complex medical procedures, certain crafting recipes) can have hard requirements based on both skill *and* the associated MACHINE stat.
- The system pairs well with the existing high-stat Minmax secret endings (many of which already require 8+ in multiple stats).
