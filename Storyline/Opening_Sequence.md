# Opening Sequence

Status: confirmed premise, details beyond this scope still open (see `Dev-Road-Map/02-Blocking-Decisions.md`, Decision 5).

## Design Intent

Modeled on Fallout: New Vegas's courier opening — the player is given a job, a role, and an inciting incident, not a backstory. The job itself should almost never come up again after this sequence, the same way New Vegas rarely references "courier" once the game proper begins. This is a normal person doing a normal job, not a chosen one. See `Game_Design_Principles.md` — Absolute Law 1 (Player Freedom & Agency Reigns Supreme) and the minimal-backstory philosophy it implies.

## Sequence

1. **The player character is a robot working as a Space Janitor**, in orbit around Jupiter.
2. The game opens mid-shift. The player has an NPC partner — also a Space Janitor — working alongside them.
3. A third party — an unnamed, unknown individual (working name: **"not-Benny"**, per the Fallout: New Vegas parallel) — arrives and steals the game's MacGuffin.
4. "Not-Benny" triggers a **compactor process**, attempting to kill the player (and succeeding against the partner).
5. **The partner does not survive.** He is a casualty of the compactor process — not a betrayer, not someone who survives to become a later plot thread.
6. The player, critically damaged, is found and repaired. They wake up in **a mechanic's garage** — the "not-Doc Mitchell's House" beat.
7. **Character creation happens at this point** — narratively framed as the mechanic's repair/rebuild of the player. Once complete, the game proper begins.

No further backstory is imposed beyond this sequence. Who the player was before this shift, if anything, is left entirely to the player.

## The MacGuffin

Tentatively **an energy drive** (open to alternatives — not locked in). It is **extremely uncommon tech due to its unique composition/make-up**, which is what makes it valuable enough for "not-Benny" to kill two Space Janitors over. Its exact composition and why it's this rare are not yet designed.

## Open Items

- "Not-Benny"'s identity, motive detail, and full character design (currently role-only).
- The partner's name and any characterization needed to make the death land emotionally.
- Whether the MacGuffin stays an energy drive or changes.
- What triggers/enables the "compactor process" mechanically and narratively.
